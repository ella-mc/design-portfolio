The Problem

Unhappy customers spending millions on add-on external solutions

Workday, a leader in Finance and HR software with millions of users, faced a major issue: it's powerful platform was too complex. Managers, now self-serving tasks once handled by HR, described the learning process as "like learning to do my own dentistry." To cope, large customers spent up to a million dollars yearly on external guidance tools, which caused performance issues.

Realizing the unsustainability of this situation, Workday conducted research and found that customers wanted an integrated solution where they could build their own guidance. Thus, Workday launched a project to create an integrated admin tool, aiming to simplify the user experience and eliminate the need for costly, external solutions.

The Goal

Design an easy to use experience that empowers Admins to effectively curate guidance for their employees.

The Approach

Thematic Sprint Approach

As the lead designer, I divided the project into smaller, focused sprints centered around specific themes. This method ensured rapid and continuous delivery. We prioritized themes based on effort, impact, and our understanding of user needs, which helped us advance towards our goal of creating an intuitive admin experience.

Key Learnings

  1. Three weeks was not long enough for what we wanted to achieve. On reflection we estimated 6 weeks for this medium piece of work
  2. Stakeholders wanted to be greater involved in sprints so we needed to identify what key moments to involve them.
  3. Engaging with Research every week wasn’t necessary. A preferred approach was to block out a working session with Research once or twice during the testing phase of the sprint

Plan & Scope

As the lead, I established weekly goals based off Google’s Design Sprint process and determined the necessary activities to achieve them, then presented my plan to the design support and wider team. 

Gather Inspiration

As a design team, we extracted the relevant users needs from previous discovery research and used them as prompts to gather internal and external product examples. 

Ideate

 We then ideated on characteristics using product examples as inspiration.

Prototype & Test

We chose to use UserTesting.com to conduct unmoderated user-testing. I was responsible for creating the prototype, defining research objectives, and developing research tasks. I prototyped and tested a user flow designed to maximize our learning.
